Design Studio I

What is space?

Redefining the Redundant Space

This installation prompts people to pause briefly on stairs they move unconsciously along. Through this, it explores how a place previously regarded merely as a passageway can acquire special meaning through human physical occupancy.

The installation is broadly divided into three sections. First, people follow footprints to gaze at a window. Next, they are instructed to circle around and linger in the space for a moment. And finally, standing footprints allow them to explore the space they have just passed through. It makes people realize that space is a place where they take time to linger, through their perception. This device demonstrates the context in which space can be defined.

01:
The Gaze

Directing Architectural Orientation

The first installation makes people stop climbing the stairs and stare silently at the window. Through this, people realize that their unconscious actions are interrupted by footprints and that they are spending time in that space.

02:
The Orbit

Dynamic Spatial Occupation

The second installation makes people circle around the space. People who are on the phone or have worries tend to hover in that spot. It leads them into the space between the stairs, forcing them to spend time in that area.

03:
Stillness

Inducing Static Suspension

The final installation prompts one to look back on their past actions. From the footprints standing still, one reflects on their past self, who used to gaze at windows and spin around. Through this, one observes themselves occupying the space in time and considers what gives context to that space.

Trials &
Failures

Iterative Behavior Modification

In the first attempt, footprints and elevator button were installed. While it succeeded in getting people to follow the footprints and look at the window, it failed to induce them to press the button. Therefore, in the second attempt, the elevator button was removed. Some people showed interest in the footprint. However, it did not keep them in the space for long. To encourage them to stay longer, I placed several poetry books. I hope that people would look at the window, open the books, and read a poem. However, people did not actually open them to read.

Through this, I realized that it is people's presence that gives a space a special context. In other words, act of staying for a certain period of time defines a place.

You made this space

Footprints are what clearly demonstrate the act of people staying. Space exists in conjunction with time. Therefore, for context to be added to a space, someone must spend time there. I wanted to explore how space can be defined through the surplus spaces that we pass by in our daily lives.
